NEUROMODULATIONS IN WORKING MEMORY

Working memory is a cognitive process by which salient sensory information is held temporarily during task execution. We were insterested in studying the role of neuromodulations as encoders of cognitive content. We present our results on field potentials recorded from the prelimbic and secondary motor cortices of 3 rats while performing a WM task.
