I am a Scientist II at the Allen Institute in Seattle, WA, developing machine learning and dynamical systems approaches for modeling complex behavioral and neural systems. My work focuses on recurrent neural networks, probabilistic and state-space modeling, representation learning, and statistical methods for extracting structure from large-scale, high-dimensional datasets. I completed my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Florida, where I developed information-theoretic and machine learning methods to characterize oscillatory dynamics in neural recordings and infer causal relationships between multi-scale biological signals and behavior. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, statistical modeling, and complex dynamical systems.
